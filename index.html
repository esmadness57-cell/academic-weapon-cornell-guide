What are Cornell Notes?

The Cornell Note-Taking System is one of the world's most effective methods for learning and remembering information. Instead of writing random notes, you organize every page into sections that improve Active Recall, revision, and long-term memory. This system is used by students, professionals, and top-performing learners around the world.

Cue Column

Keywords Questions Definitions Formulas

Notes Area

Write detailed class notes here. Include diagrams, examples, important concepts, and explanations.

How to Use Cornell Notes

01

Record

Take notes during class. Focus on concepts, examples, definitions, and diagrams.

02

Cue

After class, write keywords, questions, and important terms in the Cue column.

03

Recall

Cover the Notes section. Answer every question using memory. This builds Active Recall.

04

Reflect

Connect today's lesson with previous knowledge. Look for real-world applications.

05

Review

Spend 10–15 minutes reviewing every few days. Small reviews beat cramming.

06

Summarize

Write a concise summary at the bottom of the page. If you can't explain it, you haven't mastered it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Cornell Notes really work?

Yes. They encourage Active Recall and organized revision, which are proven learning strategies.

Should I rewrite all my notes?

No. Simply organize your notes into the Cornell format and review them regularly.

How often should I review?

Review within 24 hours, then after 3 days, 1 week, and monthly.
